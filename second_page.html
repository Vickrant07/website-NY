ToDo around New York

In case you are overwhelmed by choices in this big city, here are only a few imaginable ways you can spend your day:)

Central Park

It is located between the upper west and upper east sides of Manhattan on an area of 843 acres, which makes it the fifth-largest park in the city. With an estimated 42 million visitors annually, it is the United States' most visited urban park. It has also been filmed in more than 150 countries around the world.

The Metropolitan Museum of Art

Its permanent collection contains over 2 million works, divided among 17 curatorial departments, making it the largest museum in the United States. A major art museum, 1000 Fifth Avenue, is located on Central Park's eastern edge in Manhattan's Upper East Side along Museum Mile. It is the largest art museum in the world by area.

The Museum of Modern Art

In addition to acquiring modern and contemporary art, the MoMA (The Museum of Modern Art) produces exhibitions and film programs, as well as supporting research and publications related to its mission. Among the pieces in MoMA's collection are paintings by Alexander Calder,sculptures by Paul Cézanne, drawings by Vincent van Gogh, prints by Jasper Johns, and photographs by Jackson Pollack.

Statue of Liberty National Monument

Located in the harbor of New York City, the Statue of Liberty is a colossal neoclassical sculpture. This copper statue was designed by French sculptor Frédéric Auguste Bartholdi whose metal framework was built by Gustave Eiffel as a gift from the people of France to the people of the United States. The statue represents Libertas, a robed Roman goddess of liberty, dedicated on October 28, 1886.

Empire State Building

Named after New York's state nickname, Empire State, the building stands 1,454 feet tall, including its antenna, with a roof height of 1,250 feet. Shreve, Lamb & Harmon designed and built the 102-story Empire State Building in 1930-1931 in Midtown Manhattan, New York City, United States.

Solomon R. Guggenheim Museum

Solomon R. Guggenheim Museum, commonly called The Guggenheim, is a museum of contemporary art in the Upper East Side neighborhood of Manhattan. It is located on Fifth Avenue at East 89th Street.There are permanent collections of Impressionists, Post-Impressionists, early Moderns, and contemporary art on display here, as well as a variety of special exhibitions held throughout the year.

Times Square

Located in Midtown Manhattan at the junction of Broadway and Seventh Avenue, Times Square is a major commercial intersection, tourist attraction, entertainment venue, and neighborhood. This area, which runs from West 42nd Street to West 47th Street, is brightly illuminated with billboards and advertisements, and is often called "the crossroads of the world", "the center of the universe".

Brooklyn Bridge and Brooklyn Bridge Park

Opening in 1883, the Brooklyn Bridge was the first fixed crossing of the East River. Built in a hybrid style of cable-stayed and suspension design, it spans the East River between Manhattan and Brooklyn.

American Museum of Natural History

In New York City, on the Upper West Side, there is the American Museum of Natural History. There are 26 buildings that make up the museum complex, all connected to each other. There are 45 permanent exhibition halls, a planetarium and a library. There are more than 34 million items in the museum's collections, including fossils, animals, rocks, meteorites, human remains, and cultural artifacts from around the world. It is impossible to show all of them at once.

One World Trade Center

A World Trade Center building is the tallest building in the United States, the tallest building in the Western Hemisphere, and the seventh tallest building in the world. One World Trade Center is part of the World Trade Center complex in Lower Manhattan, New York City, which has been rebuilt.

Top of The Rock

Rockefeller Center observation deck

Intrepid Sea, Air & Space Museum

It operates a museum centered on the aircraft carrier Intrepid, the space shuttle Enterprise, jets, guided missiles, and a guided missile submarine. In addition to the A-12, a Concorde is also displayed.

St. Patrick's Cathedral

Located in Midtown Manhattan, St. Patrick's Cathedral is a church serving as both a parish and the seat of the Archbishop of New York. This cathedral is located across the street from Rockefeller Center, on a city block bound by Fifth Avenue, Madison Avenue, 50th Street, and 51st Street.

Washington Square Park

One of New York City's most popular public parks, Washington Square Park is a meeting place and cultural center in the Greenwich Village neighborhood of Lower Manhattan. Washington Square Park is home to 9,75 acres of green space.

Lincoln Center for the Performing Arts

From its 16-acre complex in New York City, Lincoln Center presents a variety of live performances including music, theater, dance, and opera. In addition to its educational programs, Lincoln Center is home to organizations such as the School of American Ballet, the New York Philharmonic, the Metropolitan Opera, and the Film Society of Lincoln Center. Each year, more than 5 million people attend more than 5,000 performances and educational programs.

Yankee Stadium

The New York Yankees play their home games in Yankee Stadium, which is located in Concourse, Bronx, New York City. It is also the venue for the Pinstripe Bowl, which is hosted annually at this stadium.

Niagara Falls

Niagara Falls, which borders New York and Canada, roars with 750,000 gallons of water falling every second.You can even sail close enough to get soaked on a boat tour.
